More

   A new face should appear, as well as some of the inner workings of how faces are built. The key is that there are sets of male and female people, each on their own representing one person. Then, there exists a separate RGB mask for each person detailing the shoulders, eyes, nose, and some other special designated colours for overlaps/voiding. These masks are applied to the original photo, acting as separate pieces of a face. These are scaled a bit and finally overlapped to form a new visage. As in the game, this same procedure is done to produce three different "photos": the actual entrant, a black and white photo, and one used in strip searches.

Face Deconstruction and Reconstruction

   Reverse engineering can be a difficult feat. You attempt to look into a black box. One can provide a meandry of inputs which correspond to a particular output. But, deducing the function at hand is, I'm pretty sure, a NP problem. This procedure intertwines science and art together; logic and deduction will get you partway until there are too many variables to juggle whilst in art, one studies the concept of form, i.e. how objects ought to look. We can easily discern whether a face looks "right" with a fraction of a glance. Thus art will be the basis for one's reasoning on how to navigate such a problem... until you bust out the statistics. I took two weeks being perpetually stumped by how facial features were scaled to make a complexion appear natural.
